Overview

Aim

Bridge the gap for young people (ages 18-28) in India who live alone/with flatmates to consume and follow a healthy food lifestyle by engaging in an efficient system which is sustainable and economical.


Young students and working professionals living in apartments located in urban areas of India struggle to follow healthy eating habits and experience poor cooking habits which leads to generating food waste and unhealthy lifestyles because they often are occupied with long working hours, cook in incorrect quantities, resort to a cheaper option in online and physical stores and lack information as to what's helpful to them. Hence, there is a need to tackle this issue in India by providing an accessible and efficient system that provides to the needs of such a target audience by ensuring the system is sustainable and efficient.

Ideation

Narrowing Opportunity Areas

Taking the insights generated forward, I then ideated using ideation tools and iterated on 2 concepts which I felt was most relevant to the problem that I was trying to solve for my target audience of ages 18-28

This concept is a service based platform which works on a subscription model that not only helps you have a better diet, consume more nutritious food but also save your energy and time by supplying best groceries on a weekly basis while you track its information, recipe, details, etc. through the app.


The idea is that usually farm producers, urban farmers growing in their personal farms, backyards, etc. and people who buy surplus food by mistake, often find it difficult to consume their food in time. This is a concept to build a bridge by helping anyone who would like to contribute their supply on this platform which is then collected by the platform in an inventory where segregation, cleaning and clustering happens. After this the inventory creates best meal boxes of homecooked recipes and distributes this to its users. If any waste generated, by the users, the waste is then collected and distributed and sent to suppliers with producing thus maintaining a circular design system where the platform takes care of the logistics.


Not only is the system community based, but also the interactions happening in the app where the people who have subscribed connect to othre farm producers, diet specialists, etc. to understand, learn and grow their healthy lifestyle.


The interaction is also aimed to be gamified, giving a sense of authority and achivement to its users and suppliers and also fun and interactive. At any time, if the person feels like skipping a meal, and going to a restaurant he can do so by simply pausing a meal, which then carries forward to the next week at no extra charges!


How this concept defines my problem statement is by valuing and then minimising the time spent on choosing, sourcing and cooking the users meals by at the same time being sustainable. Efficiency is given importance here and with also information as to habits and data on how the meals are being consumed and in what quantitites. It also makes managing personal lifestyle habits more easier with the help of resources, and information given and curated by professionals.

Visual Language

The visual language was inspired by farmer’s market where the colours are heavily influenced and aimed to reflect providing care, being friendly and being knowledgeable.


The typography used is also inspired by shapes that we see at a farmer’s markets (mostly vegetables and fruits) and developed a more curvy and circular type, hence I chose Poppins

The Logo is inspired by sustainable methods used in a farmer’s market. I have been inspired by how in a vegetable market, fruits and vegetables are segregated in compartments in boxes, and wanted to replicate something similar to that.
